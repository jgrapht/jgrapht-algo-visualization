      Glossary — modularity, local moving, aggregation, resolution limit
      Community / cluster
      A group of vertices the algorithm keeps together — denser links inside than across. Shown by colour; each connected blob of one colour is a community.
      Modularity Q
      How many edges fall inside communities versus what you'd expect by chance: Q = Σ_c [ 2·ein(c)/2m − (Σtot(c)/2m)² ]. Higher is better; maximising it is NP-hard. The stat panel tracks Q as it climbs.
      Local moving
      The greedy core: repeatedly take one vertex out of its community and put it into the neighbouring community that gives the best modularity gain, until a full pass moves nobody.
      Modularity gain
      The change in Q from moving vertex i into community C: w(i→C) − Σtot(C)·ki/2m. A vertex moves only when the best gain beats staying put.
      Aggregation
      After local moving settles, contract every community into a single super-vertex (intra-community edges become self-loops, so degrees are conserved) and run local moving again on the much smaller graph. Each round is one level.
      Local moving vs multilevel
      Local moving alone (one level) finds tight clusters but can stop at a fine-grained partition. Multilevel — JGraphT's LouvainClustering — keeps aggregating, which can merge those clusters into coarser communities of higher modularity and scales to millions of edges.
      Σtot
      Total weighted degree of all vertices currently in a community — the term that penalises large communities in the modularity gain.
      2m
      Twice the number of edges (the sum of all degrees). JGraphT's LouvainClustering uses the same 2m / self-loop conventions as UndirectedModularityMeasurer, so getModularity() agrees exactly.
      Resolution limit
      Modularity can fail to separate very small communities, sometimes merging them — a known limitation Louvain inherits, and a motivation for newer methods (Leiden, etc.).
      What you're watching — Louvain community detection

      Pick either mode in each pane

      Two views of JGraphT's org.jgrapht.alg.clustering.LouvainClustering run on the same graph:

      • Local moving (1 level) — only the greedy local-moving phase on the original graph. Fast, and often enough, but it stops at the first stable partition.
      • Multilevel (+ aggregation) — the full algorithm: after local moving settles, communities are contracted into super-vertices and local moving runs again, level after level, until nothing improves.

      The punchline: on well-separated graphs both reach the same partition (the planted example), so aggregation is "free" insurance. But on a hierarchical graph, local moving alone gets stuck at the fine clusters while the multilevel run aggregates them into coarser communities of strictly higher modularity. Compare the communities and Q stats.
